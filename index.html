Urgent Roof Request Guidance

Some roof problems need quick attention, but safety comes first.

If there is electrical danger, structural collapse risk, fire, injury, active flooding near electrical fixtures, or immediate danger, contact emergency services first. If it is safe, move valuables away from active drips and place buckets or containers indoors.

Do not climb onto a wet, icy, steep, damaged, or unsafe roof. Photos from the ground or from a safe indoor location are usually safer than roof access.

Common Roof Problems in Princeton Homes

Local roofs can face heavy rain, snow, wind, freeze-thaw movement, and seasonal wear.

Leak and moisture clues

  • Water stains on ceilings or walls
  • Active dripping during rain or snow melt
  • Attic moisture or damp insulation
  • Leaks around chimneys, vents, skylights, flashing, or pipe boots

Exterior damage clues

  • Missing, lifted, curled, or damaged shingles
  • Metal roof concerns or fastener issues
  • Storm debris, branch impact, or wind damage
  • Openings where temporary tarp help may be requested
