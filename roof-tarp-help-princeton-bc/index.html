When People Request Roof Tarp Help

Use this page when temporary protection is the main concern.

  • Active water entry after rain, snow, wind, or storm damage
  • Missing shingles, open areas, punctures, or visible roof openings
  • Branch damage, debris damage, or lifted materials
  • Temporary protection questions before a full repair decision
  • Concern that more rain or snow may worsen the leak

Do not climb on unsafe roofs

Do not climb onto a wet, icy, steep, damaged, or unsafe roof. If there is electrical danger, structural risk, fire, injury, flooding near electrical fixtures, or immediate danger, contact emergency services first.

Roof Tarp Help FAQ

Can I request temporary roof tarp help?

Yes. You can submit a request. Provider availability, roof access, safety, pricing, and suitability vary.

Is tarping a permanent repair?

No. Tarping may be temporary protection only. The provider handles diagnosis and repair recommendations.

Should I install a tarp myself?

Avoid unsafe roof access. Do not climb onto a wet, icy, steep, damaged, or unsafe roof.
